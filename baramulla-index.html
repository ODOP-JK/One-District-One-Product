Baramulla

Baramulla, a picturesque town nestled in the Indian-administered union territory of Jammu and Kashmir, is celebrated for its apple orchards and the high-quality apples it yields. These apples serve as the foundation for a range of delectable apple-based products. From the refreshing allure of freshly squeezed apple juice to the sweet nostalgia of apple jams and preserves, the local apples find their way into an array of culinary delights.

Apple Juice: Baramulla apples are often used to make fresh apple juice, which is a popular and refreshing beverage. It can be found in local markets and may be sold by local juice vendors.

Apple Jam and Preserves: Local producers often create delicious apple jams and preserves using Baramulla apples. These can be enjoyed on bread, toast, or as accompaniments to various dishes.

Dried Apples: Apples can be sliced and dried to make apple chips or dried apple slices, which are convenient and healthy snacks. Look for these in local markets or specialty stores.
